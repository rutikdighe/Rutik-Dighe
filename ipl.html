TATA IPL TEAM

Rohit Sharma

Rohit Sharma is a player that needs no introduction; he is the most successful player in the history of the league, having won six IPL titles, one with Deccan Chargers in 2009 and five as captain of Mumbai Indians (2013, 2015, 2017, 2019, 2020), making him the joint most successful captain in the league. Not just with his leadership, Rohit Sharma has also been instrumental with the bat and is currently the third-highest run-scorer in the history of the tournament. He also has the distinction of being the only player to score three ODI double-centuries and most recently also led India to back-to-back ICC trophies (the 2024 T20 World Cup and the 2025 Champions Trophy).

Surya Kumar Yadav

Surya Kumar Yadav came to the spotlight with his performance at the 2011-12 Ranji Trophy season where he topped the charts for Mumbai scoring 754 runs in 9 games. The same year, Surya Kumar was picked by Mumbai Indians. In 2013, he moved to KKR where he played a crucial role in the middle order scoring quick runs and was part of the team that won the trophy in 2014. In 2018, SKY moved back to MI and had a breakthrough season scoring 512 runs. He was also instrumental in the team’s success in 2019 and 2020 which eventually saw him get a national team call-up. Since then there has been no looking back for Surya Kumar Yadav, who has become a consistent performer for both his IPL franchise and the Indian team and was part of the team that won the 2024 T20 World Cup. Surya currently is also the captain of the Indian National T20 side taking over the role after Rohit Sharma’s retirement from the format. His ability to score quickly with his innovative stroke play and deliver consistently saw him being retained by MI for TATA IPL 2025.

robin minz

An explosive wicketkeeper batter from Jharkhand, Robin Minz impressed everyone with his power-hitting abilities in local tournaments. The left-hander was picked by Gujarat Titans at the IPL 2024 Auction. This time at the 2025 TATA IPL Auction, the southpaw was roped in by Mumbai Indians at INR 65 Lakh.

ryan rickelton

A power-packed hitter also known for his wicket-keeping skills, Ryan Rickelton is a South African international who has gained the spotlight, having displayed scintillating form across various formats in recent times. The player was a part of South Africa’s 2024 T20 World Cup squad, where they finished runners-up, and most recently scored a 103-run knock for his team during their Champions Trophy 2025 opener against Afghanistan. The left hander attracted attention due to his ability to score quickly and was picked by MI for INR 1 Crore at the 2025 TATA IPL Auction.

shrijit krishnan

Shrijith Krishnan is an attacking wicket-keeper batter from Karnataka who, in recent years, has become a regular in the domestic circuit. The left-handed batter scored a century on his Ranji Trophy debut and was a key part of Karnataka’s triumphant 2024-25 Vijay Hazare Trophy campaign, scoring an important 78 in the final. Shrijith was acquired by MI for INR 30 Lakh at the 2025 TATA IPL Auction. The experience of rubbing shoulders with some of the best in the world will help him grow as a player.

bevon jacobs

Born in South Africa, Bevon Jacobs is a powerful top-order batter who plays for New Zealand. Striking close to 150, Jacobs is an exciting young talent to keep an eye on. On the back of consistent performances in domestic cricket, he was picked by MI for INR 30 Lakh at the 2025 TATA IPL Auction.

N. tilak varma

Tilak Varma is a talented left-handed batter from Hyderabad who rose through the domestic ranks. He was picked by Mumbai Indians at the IPL 2022 auction and was straightaway drafted into the team, making an instant impact, scoring 397 runs at an average of 36.09 and a strike rate of 131.02. He bettered that performance the next year, scoring 343 at an average of 42.88, striking at 164.11, a performance that also earned him a national debut. In IPL 2024, Tilak put up a destructive performance, scoring 416 runs. The player was retained by MI ahead of the 2025 TATA IPL 2025 Auction and comes in red-hot form recently having become the first ever player to score three successive 100s in T20s.

ALL ROUNDERS

hardik pandya

Hardik Pandya began his IPL Career with Mumbai Indians where he spent seven seasons winning titles in 2015, 2017, 2019 and 2020. He was picked by Gujarat Titans in 2022 and named captain. He started off with a bang, leading them to a title win in their very first season. He continued to display his leadership abilities in the team’s second season, guiding them to the final. Following two successful seasons with GT, Hardik moved to MI and was appointed captain. A terrific utility player Pandya can smash sixes at will and change the course of matches on his own. An impact player and a match-winner, Pandya has delivered crucial performances with the ball and can bowl in all the phases of the game. The Baroda all-rounder has been a vital cog for the Indian national team at the international stage both with the bat and the ball, playing several match-winning innings including some high-stakes performances during India’s triumph at the 2024 T20 World Cup and Champions Trophy 2025. His all-round abilities make him an asset across all formats and thus has been retained as MI’s captain for TATA IPL 2025.

naman dhir

A top-order batting allrounder from Punjab, Naman Dhir strikes over 150 in T20 cricket while also having an economy under 8. He was a part of the Punjab team that won the Syed Mushtaq Ali Trophy 2023/24. He was picked up by Mumbai Indians for IPL 2024, where he put in some interesting performances, scoring at a strike rate of 177.22, which saw him spark a bidding war at the 2025 TATA IPL 2025 Auction before being acquired by MI via RTM for INR 5.25 Crore.

will jacks

English batting all-rounder Will Jacks is a very handy player to have in your team. With a strike rate of 150+, he has shown that he is a destructive batter who can also occasionally give you a few overs with the ball. Jacks secured a six-wicket inning haul on his Test debut and has managed to represent England across all three formats. He was handed an IPL contract by Royal Challengers Bengaluru in 2023 and later went on to score 230 runs at a strike rate of 175.57 in IPL 2024 including a match-winning century as well. Jacks will now represent Mumbai Indians in TATA IPL 2025 after being roped in for INR 5.25 Crore.

mitchell santner

A left-arm spinner, Mitchell Santner has been a regular in the IPL, spending seven years at Chennai Super Kings and winning two titles in-process. Mitchell Santner has also been a consistent performer for New Zealand at the international level and led his side to finish runners-up at the 2025 ICC Champions Trophy. He was signed by MI for INR 2 Crore. Mitchell Santner is also very handy with the bat and is capable of playing a quick knock.

raj angad bawa

Raj Angad Bawa was one of the key members during India’s triumph at the 2022 U-19 World Cup. The youngster was effective with both the bat and the ball, scoring 252 runs in just five innings, including a whirlwind knock of 162* against Uganda while picking 9 wickets, including a fifer in the final against England. The Himachali, following his performances, was picked by PBKS for IPL 2022 and will now don the Blue and Gold after being picked by MI for INR 30 Lakh.

vighnesh puthur

Vignesh Puthur is a young left-arm wrist spinner from Kerala. He attracted the scouts with his unique action and was roped in by Mumbai Indians for INR 30 Lakh at the 2025 TATA IPL Auction before even making his debut for Kerala in domestic cricket. It will be a great learning curve for the youngsters to play alongside the best in the business.

corbin bosch

South African all-rounder Corbin Bosch came to the limelight after a POTM spell of 4/15 in the U19 World Cup 2014 final that helped his side lift the title. He also has the ability to hit huge sixes which makes him a vital cog in T20 cricket. The all-rounder put up a match-winning performance on his Test debut with an important 81* run knock and a five-wicket match-haul. He was a part of the Rajasthan Royals setup earlier and has been drafted in by Mumbai Indians for TATA IPL 2025 to replace injured Lizaad Williams.

BOWLERS

trent boult

Trent Boult is one of the most prolific pacers in T20 cricket. He first rose to the spotlight following his achievements at the 2015 World Cup, which saw him end as the joint-highest wicket-taker with 22 wickets. Over the years, he has represented SRH, KKR, DC, MI, and RR, winning two titles, first with SRH in 2016 and then with MI in 2020, where he picked 25 wickets, including a Player of the Match performance in the Final. The left-arm pacer can swing the new ball and is known for striking early in the powerplay consistently. Boult is back in the MI fold after being secured for a price of INR 12.5 Crore at the 2025 TATA IPL Auction.

karn sharma

A leg spinner hailing from Uttar Pradesh, Karn Sharma is a seasoned campaigner when it comes to the IPL, having represented Sunrisers Hyderabad, Mumbai Indians, Chennai Super Kings, and Royal Challengers Bengaluru in the past and has won three titles. His consistent performances in his first two seasons also saw him get a national team call-up. Karn Sharma, during his previous stint with the men in Blue and Gold, saw him pick 13 wickets in nine innings, and this time around, the leggie was picked by MI for INR 50 Lakh.

deepak chahar

Deepak Chahar came to recognition when he picked up eight wickets in an innings during his Ranji Trophy debut. Since then, there has been no looking back for the skillful pacer. Chahar made his IPL debut for Rising Pune Supergiant before spending the next seven years at CSK, where he played the role of a lead bowler, especially in the powerplay. He impressed under the captaincy of Dhoni and received the national call-up in limited-overs cricket. Deepak Chahar has three IPL trophies to his name and will now play for MI after being picked at 9.25 Crore.

ashwani kumar

Ashwani Kumar is a young left-arm fast bowler from Punjab. He garnered attention with his ability to bowl brilliantly in the slog overs. He has plenty of variations in his kitty which makes him a promising asset. MI roped him in for INR 30 Lakh at the 2025 TATA IPL Auction.

reece topley

Reece Topley is a tall left-arm pacer who can bowl early in the powerplay and can also bowl lethal yorkers in the slog overs. Topley has been making headlines since his U19 days, finishing the U19 World Cup 2012 as the highest wicket-taker with 19 wickets. He has represented England in over 60 matches across the white ball format with the most notable performance being his six-wicket haul in an ODI game against India. After two seasons with RCB in 2023 and 2024, Topley will play for MI in TATA IPL 2025 after being acquired at INR 75 Lakh. With a lot of international experience under his belt, he will be handy in case the team wants another overseas pacer to link up with Trent Boult.

V. satyanarayana penmetsa

A right-arm fast bowler native of Andhra, Satyanarayana is coming up the ranks having played all three formats in domestic cricket. Mumbai Indians roped him in for INR 30 Lakh for TATA IPL 2025 where he will get to share the dressing room with greats of the game.

arjun tendulkar

Arjun Tendulkar has been a part of Mumbai Indians since 2021. The left-arm pacer is not just handy with the ball but is also capable of adding some valuable runs with the bat. He plays for Goa in the domestic arena. At the TATA IPL 2025 auction, Arjun was bought back by MI for INR 30 Lakh.

mujeeb-ur-rahman

Mujeeb-ur-Rahman has been picked by Mumbai Indians as a replacement for Allah Ghazanfar at INR 2 Crore. An off-break bowler, Mujeeb previously played for Punjab Kings and Kolkata Knight Riders. The player has also put up some spell-binding performances for the Afghan national team and MI will look forward to the player’s performances especially in spin friendly conditions.

jasprit bumrah

Jasprit Bumrah has been with MI from the start of his career, making his debut in the 2013 season. Bumrah has been instrumental for the Mumbai franchise as he finished as the team's highest wicket-taker in three of their IPL title wins (2017, 2019, and 2020). For both the franchise and the Indian National team, Bumrah has established himself as the mainstay and one of the most dependable fast bowlers across all formats and in any phase of the game. His unique action makes it tough for the batters to pick him early whereas his deadly yorkers and deceptive slower deliveries add icing on the cake. He was also named the ‘Player of the Series’ for India at the 2024 T20I World Cup. Bumrah was retained ahead of TATA IPL 2025 and will enter the season after being named the ICC Men’s Cricketer of the Year 2024.
